Internal Trade

This is the buying and selling of goods and services within the confined boundaries of the nation. The products being purchased from the neighbouring local shop or from a central market or from departmental shops or from malls or even from door-to-door selling all come under internal trade.

Wholesale Trade

Wholesale trade consists of purchasing and selling goods, generally to retailers, professional users or authorities, or to other wholesalers or intermediaries, in large quantities. Wholesale trade intermediaries put buyers and vendors into contact without being owners of the goods.

Retail Trade

Retail Trade involves selling of goods and services to the ultimate consumers. A retailer purchases goods in large quantities from manufacturers, directly or through a wholesaler, and then sells in smaller quantities to consumers.

Fixed Store Trade

The name itself explains that they do not move from one place to another. They can establish a medium or a large scale business in a fixed place. They do not move from place to place to serve their customers.

Departmental Store

A department store is a retail establishment offering a wide range of consumer goods in different areas of the store, each area specializing in a product category.
